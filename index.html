What You'll Learn

Overview

CORe consists of three courses—Business Analytics, Economics for Managers, and Financial Accounting —and a final exam. You will master concepts as you tackle multimedia case studies of diverse organizations, develop business intuition through interactive learning exercises, and join a global cohort of peers on the HBX course platform. As in the business world, you’ll be evaluated on the quality and timeliness of your work and your contributions to the learning community.

Business Analytics

  • Develop a data mindset
  • Analyze and interpret data to inform business decisions
  • Starting with basic descriptive statistics course progresses to regression analysis
  • Implement analytical techniques in Excel
  • Apply course concepts to A/B testing on a website, using sampling to check warehouse inventory, predicting sales, and forecasting staffing needs

Professor Jan Hammond teaches in the Technology and Operations Management (TOM) unit at HBS and serves as Senior Associate Dean for Culture and Community. She currently teaches Supply Chain Management in the MBA program. Her research focuses on speed and flexibility in manufacturing and logistics systems to respond to changing customer demand.

Economics for Managers

  • Learn the power of economic thinking to drive business decisions
  • Develop effective pricing strategies
  • Understand powerful analytical tools like relative cost analysis and conjoint analysis
  • Identify sources of competitive advantage through differentiation
  • Apply course concepts in the context of a growing online retailer, an events ticket broker, a multi-national publishing company, and other firms

Professor Bharat Anand teaches in the Strategy unit at HBS and also serves as Senior Associate Dean, HBX. He has studied how new technologies affect what we watch, read, and hear, and how companies navigate digital change. His book The Content Trap: A Strategist’s Guide to Digital Change was released in 2016.

Financial Accounting

  • Unlock insights into business performance and potential through financial statements
  • Understand both US GAAP and IFRS accounting standards
  • Prepare and evaluate financial forecasts
  • Value a venture, project, or investment opportunity and perform a sensitivity analysis
  • See how managers, Wall Street analysts, and entrepreneurs use accounting to drive strategic decision making.

Professor V.G. Narayanan teaches in the Accounting and Management unit at HBS and serves as faculty chair of the MBA program’s second-year, elective curriculum. He has served as course head of the first-year required accounting course for MBA students. His research in management accounting focuses on understanding how firms can use incentives and performance feedback to improve performance.
